Income & debt i We use your income and monthly debt payments to calculate your debt-to-income ratio (DTI). DTI is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes toward debt payments. Lenders use it to measure how much of a mortgage payment you can realistically handle. Lower DTI = more buying power and better loan options. Most lenders prefer to see a DTI under 43%, with under 36% being ideal.
